Meet Our Panelists

Join us on May 13th to hear these incredible educators and education experts speak about the ways in which your teachers and schools can move from piloting blended learning to a in-depth immersion into blended learning. If you are unable to attend in person, be sure to tune into our live stream of the event. 

   Allyson O’Rourke Barrett, History Instructor at the Pre-Collegiate Learning Center 
   Elizabeth Pape, Online Education Consultant
   Jennifer Levy, Chief Academic Officer of Magen David Yeshivah High School
   John Watson, Founder of the Evergreen Education Group 

Allyson O’Rourke Barrett, History Instructor at the Pre-Collegiate Learning Center 

Allyson is a graduate of Smith College with a B.A. in English Language and Literature.  She began teaching in New Orleans while working on Hurricane Katrina relief efforts, and taught at University of Massachusetts Amherst and Elite Preparatory Academy before joining the history department at the PCLC.  Allyson specializes in the history and literature of World War II, the Nantucket whaling industry, and the analysis of primary source material.  When she is not teaching, Allyson enjoys cooking, creative writing, and playing with her dog Bailey.

 

 

 

Elizabeth Pape, Online Education Consultant

Liz Pape has nearly twenty years of experience in K-12 online education. For ten years, Liz was the CEO of The VHS Collaborative a non-profit collaborative of high schools across the U.S. and abroad offering Internet-based courses to high school students in VHS member schools. She has a B.A in Economics and an MBA. For the past twenty years she has been involved in education, as a technology specialist, as the grant administrator for the Virtual High School Technology Innovation Challenge Grant, and as the CEO of VHS.

Liz has shared her expertise in K-12 online education, speaking nationally at numerous education and technology conferences, including the National Education Summit, the American Association of School Administrators, the Association of Supervisors and Curriculum Developers, the National School Board Association and National Educational Computing Conference and has written nationally about online education. She has served on the U.S. Department of Education National Online Education Policy Forum, the NCREL Blue Ribbon Panelist for Online Education, the Blackboard K-12 Advisory Board and is a founding board member of the North American Council for Online Learning (NACOL).

In 2005, Liz received the Community Builder Award from the National Coalition for Technology in Education and Training, in recognition of her efforts in advancing the use of technology in education. VHS has received the USDLA 21st Century Best Practices Award, the Stockholm Challenge Award 2001 for innovative use of technology in education, and Magna2000 School Award for outstanding programs that advance student learning.

Jennifer Levy, Chief Academic Officer of Magen David Yeshivah High School 

Jenni Levy is the Chief Academic Officer of Magen David Yeshivah High School and a licensed attorney in the State of New Jersey.  She spends most of her day working with the curriculum, evaluating and working with teachers to improve their instruction, and analyzing data from assessment to improve student learning.  Additionally, she is licensed in both social studies teaching K-12 and Elementary Education.   She has spent over 10 years as a classroom teacher in both public and private school and Hebrew School teacher at a variety of synagogues in the tri-state area.  She holds a bachelor from Stern College, Yeshiva University and a master’s degree from Teachers College, Columbia University.  She is ABD in PhD from Bernard Revel Graduate School, Yeshiva University and completed over 30 credits of course work at the Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, Columbia University towards this goal.  She holds a JD from Fordham Law School.   She is an avid and award winning blogger who writes extensively on the use of educational technology and best classroom practices at www.jennilevyesq.blogspot.com .  Previously, she owned a test prep company, Bulldog Test Prep, LLC, which she opened with her husband, Daniel Levy in 2006 serving over 400 students in the tri-state area.   She and her family live in Teaneck, NJ.  In addition, she is a founding board member of Yeshivat He’Atid in Bergenfield, NJ.

John Watson, Founder of the Evergreen Education Group

John Watson, founder of the Evergreen Education Group, started consulting in 2000 after serving as director of business development and consultant for eCollege, where he helped educational organizations launch their online programs and was instrumental in developing the company’s K-12 strategy. John and Evergreen’s work has been cited in the New York Times, Education Week, and eSchool News, he has appeared on NBC Nightly News, and he has presented numerous times at the Virtual School Symposium of the International Association for K-12 Online Learning, Technology and Leadership Conference of the National School Boards Association, and other conferences. Prior to online learning, John worked in environmental consulting, environmental education, and a variety of other education, environmental, and travel-related fields. John holds a BA from Middlebury College and an MS and MBA from the University of Michigan.
