Hands On Learning in a “Digital Sandbox”

August 13, 2015 | By Tatyana Dvorkin, Associate Director, DigitalJLearning Network

We talk a lot at DJLN about blended and online learning, and a lot about Middle and Upper School, but for this post, I’d like to take it back to the basics. Because before our students learn about digital citizenship, or typing, or chemistry, or chumash, they all learn how to count, read, write, and all those delightful basics of early childhood learning.

Tiggly, a New York-based company founded in 2012, develops learning tools for exactly these skills, that combine physical manipulatives and digital applications. Physical manipulatives have long been highlighted by constructivists as a vital part of early childhood learning and Tiggly draws on this theory to place kids in a digital playground of sorts. Tiggly produces apps and manipulatives for learning shapes, counting, colors, reading, and are working on more apps that will engage children’s imagination while teaching other vital skills.

Yonah Kirschner and I paid a visit to Tiggly’s studios last week to see where the magic happens. We got a tour of their sunny studio space at WeWork, met the team, saw the hands-on store displays and got to sit down for a chat with Chief Learning Officer and co-founder Azadeh Jamalian. She shared with us some basics about Tiggly and about what is coming down the pipeline this year.

Tiggly Toys are made of plastic and silicone and use capacitive touch point technology to have the tablet respond to the toys the same way it usually does to fingertips. Tiggly currently produces 3 different toy sets: Tiggly Shapes, which uses animal themes and drawing to teach kids ages 2-5 about circles, triangles, squares, and stars; Tiggly Math, which focuses on basic math through an animated cooking application, cartoon making, and a side-scroller game for ages 3-7; and Tiggly Words, which teaches vowel sounds and reinforces basic reading skills to kids 4-8 years old. Each toy set can be used with three different free apps. There are more toys being developed! We got a bit of a sneak peak and are excited for what’s coming down the pipeline.

Currently, Tiggly Toys are available for consumer purchase, and are being tested by classrooms around the country to help build a curriculum for teachers. This September, Tiggly will launch their education edition in schools nationwide, at a slightly lower cost and with instructional materials included. Tiggly also has a “Buy one, give one” program in which they send a free Tiggly Toys set to a school of your choice when you buy one on their website.

It’s exciting to see this unique combination of the physical and digital and imagine what kind of potential learning opportunities can come when the benefits of the two platforms combine. A recent study by independent research firm, PlayCollective, has show that playing with the Tiggly Math program resulted in an increase in early number skills in up to 71% of kids who played with the toys for two weeks. In a blended learning format, we think Tiggly’s tools could certainly be used in the Early Learning, Kindergarten, or Lower School classrooms as a rotation tool. Stations set up with tablets and the toys could be used by students who need some additional supplemental work or who learn best through a creative format.

DJLN would like to thank Azadeh and the entire team at Tiggly for letting us check out their space and taking the time to chat with us!
