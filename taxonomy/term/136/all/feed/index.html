So when Chris Walsh, in the session on The Power of Do at the 2014 ISTE conference, asked for volunteers to play with the original Mr. Potato Head, my hand quickly shot up. Even though I was not one of the volunteers selected, the activity that he demonstrated has stayed with me since I left the conference.  Instead of the traditional round plastic toy with openings placed in strategic parts of the frame for the various body parts, Chris handed the volunteers an actual potato along with cut up vegetables and instructed volunteers to imagine their own version of a Mr. Potato Head beyond what the toy companies set as the fixed model. Chris’s message was clear: Learning by doing is the only learning that sticks. </p> </div></div></div> <div class="field field-name-taxonomy-wp-blog-tags field-type-taxonomy-term-reference
